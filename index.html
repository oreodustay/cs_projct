Ada Lovelace

Milestone 1 - Holding The Title As The "First Programmer"

Ada Lovelace translated an article by Luigi Menabrea in 1843 about an Analytical Engine.
While doing so, Lovelace wrote expanded notes and labeled them from A to G. These notes
were several times longer than the actual translation. Within note G, it detailed precise,
step-by-step sequences of operations for the Analytical Engine to calculate a sequence of
Bernoulli numbers. (Bernoulli numbers are often used to represent variables in formulas and
mathematics.)
This achievement was recognised globally, and Lovelace was given the title of the first
programmer to publish computer algorithms for machines.

Analytical Engine definition: A 19th-century mechanical computer designed by Charles Babbage.
It became the first programmable machine, which featured memory and a processor.
